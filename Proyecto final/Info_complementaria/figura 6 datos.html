Fig. 6. Direct cultivation of bacteria from a biopsy of the distal esophagus. A biopsy of » 1 mm2 mucosal surface, representing 1 mm3 in volume, was homogenized with a sterile tissue grinder into suspension in 0.6 ml of 0.9% sodium chloride. This suspension was inoculated onto nine blood agar plates and cultured at 37°C in aerobic, microaerobic (5% CO2), or anaerobic environments. More than 2,000 colonies grew on each plate, predominantly a -hemolytic Gram-positive cocci, consistent with Streptococcus species. A representative plate is shown.
